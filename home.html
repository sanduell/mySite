Hello, fellow students!

This is an example text written originally in English. It aims to emphasize the quality of the translation in hopes of being understandable. The text content therefore is of a lesser importance, so reading it really serves no purpose, but thanks for doing so anyway. The translation needs not only to be understandable, but has to make sense to native speakers. This is a gripe which many often link to automatic translation such as Google Translate or DeepL. Typically it is noted that such translators don't catch idioms or common phrases, which literally translated don't strike one as sensible. Now, if I were to say that solving a problem is like killing two birds with one stone, I am not actually killing birds. An automatic translator might not catch this falsehood and will leave readers confused who are unaccustomed to English and reliant on the translation. This highlights the importance of manual translation or in the least proofreading but also show that AI still needs to make advancements. I will now close my rambling.

A common saying might be:

"like killing two birds with one stone"

-> (meaning: achieving two goals or solving two problems at the same time )
